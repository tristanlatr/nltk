SimpleGoodTuring ProbDist approximates from frequency to frequency of frequency into a linear line under log space by linear regression. Details of Simple Good-Turing algorithm can be found in: - Good Turing smoothing without tears" (Gale & Sampson 1995), Journal of Quantitative Linguistics, vol. 2 pp. 217-237. - "Speech and Language Processing (Jurafsky & Martin), 2nd Edition, Chapter 4.5 p103 (log(Nc) = a + b*log(c)) - http://www.grsampson.net/RGoodTur.html Given a set of pair (xi, yi), where the xi denotes the frequency and yi denotes the frequency of frequency, we want to minimize their square variation. E(x) and E(y) represent the mean of xi and yi. - slope: b = sigma ((xi-E(x)(yi-E(y))) / sigma ((xi-E(x))(xi-E(x))) - intercept: a = E(y) - b.E(x)

def _renormalize(self, r, nr): (source)

It is necessary to renormalize all the probability estimates to ensure a proper probability distribution results. This can be done by keeping the estimate of the probability mass for unseen items as N(1)/N and renormalizing all the estimates for previously seen items (as Gale and Sampson (1995) propose). (See M&S P.213, 1999)
